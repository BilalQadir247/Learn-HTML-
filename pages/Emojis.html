Using Emojis in HTML

What Are Emojis?

Emojis may look like small images, but they are actually text characters.

They are part of the Unicode (UTF-8) character set.

😀 😁 😂

Unicode (UTF-8) includes thousands of symbols, characters, and emojis from around the world.

Important Requirement

To properly display emojis on a webpage, you must set the character encoding to UTF-8 inside the <head> section:

<meta charset="UTF-8">

Without this setting, some emojis may not display correctly.

Using Emojis in HTML

You can add emojis in two ways:

<p> I love coding 😄 </p>

Using Unicode Number (Entity Code)

<p> &#128516; </p>

Both methods will display the emoji.
